# RingByRing Competitor Research

All claims in this document are sourced from real reviews, vendor pricing pages,
and public documentation. Each section ends with sources for verification.

RingByRing sits at $149/month flat (self-serve) in an AI receptionist market that
ranges from $14/mo at the floor (AI-Receptionist.com) to $2,000+/month at the
ceiling (Ruby premium, Smith.ai human plans).

The market splits into three layers:

1. **AI receptionists** (Goodcall, Rosie, Dialzara, My AI Front Desk, etc.):
   $29–$249/mo, no human in the loop, predictable flat pricing.
2. **Hybrid AI + human** (Smith.ai AI Receptionist): $95/mo+, AI handles routine
   calls, humans backup for complex ones.
3. **Human virtual receptionist services** (Ruby, Smith.ai Virtual, Posh,
   AnswerForce, AnswerConnect): $235–$2,000+/mo, real humans answering.

Below the entire market sits the status quo: voicemail, part-time hires, family
members. Most small service businesses live there, not in this market.

## AI Receptionist Competitors (direct category)

### Goodcall

**Positioning:** Budget-to-mid AI receptionist with strong workflow
configurability. Founded 2021. Used by 42,000+ businesses per their site
(Goodcall.com, May 2026).

**Verified pricing (direct from Goodcall.com/pricing, May 2026):**
- Starter: $79/month ($66 annual) — 100 unique customers/month, 1 logic flow,
  3 team members, 7 days call history
- Growth: $129/month ($108 annual) — 250 unique customers, 3 flows, 9 team
  members, 30 days history
- Scale: $249/month ($208 annual) — 500 unique customers, 25 flows, 50 team
  members, unlimited history
- Overage: $0.50/unique customer over plan cap
- Enterprise: custom

**Pricing model is unusual:** charges per unique caller phone number per month,
not per minute or per call. Repeat callers within a month don't count again.
Robocalls and quick hang-ups don't count. Unlimited minutes and tokens on every
plan. This is genuinely different from minute-based competitors and predictable
when call patterns are stable.

**Verified strengths:**
- "Workflow configurability is genuinely superior at this price point" — drag-
  and-drop builder lets non-technical operators define routing logic per caller
  type, time of day, inquiry category (ALM Corp, March 2026)
- HIPAA-compliant call flows available — "one of the few platforms in its price
  range to offer compliance documentation" (ALM Corp, March 2026)
- Multilingual support
- Google Business Profile integration: scans your GBP at setup to ingest
  address, hours, services (ServiceAgent review, January 2026)
- 14-day free trial, no contracts

**Verified weaknesses:**
- "Slightly robotic feel with higher latency than newer Gen-3 AI agents"
  (Dialora review, January 2026)
- Cannot port existing business numbers — assigns a Goodcall number, then
  requires conditional call forwarding setup (ServiceAgent, January 2026)
- "Template-based platforms like Thoughtly and Goodcall struggled with mid-call
  changes" e.g. rescheduling an appointment twice in one conversation (Retell
  AI testing, April 2026)
- CRM integration is via Zapier only — no native CRM (Goodcall FAQ, May 2026)
- Customer support: "ironically, hard to get a real person on the phone for
  help" (Dialora review citing G2/Trustpilot patterns, January 2026)
- $0.50/extra unique caller "go up rapidly when you have more than 500 distinct
  callers" (Dialora review)

### Rosie AI

**Positioning:** AI answering service targeting small local businesses, with
explicit vertical pages for plumbing, HVAC, electrical, dental, legal, real
estate, accounting, property management. Claims 1,500+ businesses and 1.4M+
calls handled per their site (Rosie/Prospeo review, 2026).

**Verified pricing — but with conflicts:**
- Apple App Store listing (verified March 2026): Professional $49/mo,
  Scale $149/mo, Growth $299/mo, with 7-day free trial
- Multiple third-party sources cite different tier names: Starter $49,
  Professional $99, Scale $149 with 250/500/1,000 minute bundles
- $0.25/minute overage cited in CallBird comparison (January 2026)

**Verified strengths:**
- Bilingual English/Spanish on all plans at no extra cost
- 7-day full-feature free trial
- Direct Google Calendar and Calendly integration on Scale plan and higher
- Mobile app (iOS/Android) for management
- Trains by scanning your website and Google Business Profile at setup

**Verified weaknesses:**
- "Appointment booking and call transfers are locked behind the $149/month plan
  — a steep jump for a feature most businesses need on day one" (Prospeo)
- No native CRM integration — Zapier only
- Requires existing phone system, works via conditional call forwarding
- English and Spanish only — no other languages
- Pricing structure conflicts unresolved publicly

## Status Quo Alternatives

### Voicemail

**The dominant default.** Used by the majority of small service businesses.

**Verified impact:**
- "85% of callers who reach voicemail never call back, and 62% will call a competitor instead"
- For home services contractors, "74.1% went completely unanswered" across 130,175 customer service calls analyzed

### Hiring a Part-Time Receptionist

**Verified costs (BLS data, 2025):**
- Receptionist median wage: $17.90/hour ($37,230/year full-time)
- Part-time at 20 hrs/week: ~$1,500–$2,800/month all-in including payroll taxes
- Full-time receptionist all-in cost: $3,050–$5,800/month

## RingByRing Positioning

**The three price bands:**
- **Budget AI:** ~$25–$65/mo, usually call/minute caps, thinner features
- **Flat-rate full-feature:** ~$149–$299/mo, unlimited calls
- **Human-hybrid:** ~$235+/mo, per-call overages $7–$11

**RingByRing at $149/mo flat, unlimited, $0 setup:**
- Sits just under the $199 NextPhone value anchor
- Above the crowded, capped $99-and-below budget tier
- Self-demoing: prospect calls and *hears* RingByRing in 30 seconds
- Gap-coverage framing (after-hours/overflow/weekend), not "replace your receptionist"

**The economic case vs. status quo:**
- If a business misses 10 calls/month at 30% conversion at $300 average ticket,
  that's $900/month in recovered revenue at full conversion
- Even at 50% effectiveness, RingByRing pays for itself

## Voice Technology Note

RingByRing uses Gemini Live API for native audio-to-audio conversation, avoiding
the latency penalty of chained STT→LLM→TTS pipelines that most budget AI
receptionists use. This enables:
- Sub-500ms latency for natural conversational flow
- Barge-in support — caller can interrupt mid-response
- Affective dialogue — model adapts tone to caller's expression
